FUNDAMENTALS OF COMPUTER ENGINEERING

The primary objective of the "Fundamentals of Computer Engineering" course is to provide a comprehensive understanding
of Computer Engineering from a global perspective, encompassing both academic and professional dimensions. This course
is designed to equip students with foundational knowledge rooted in disciplines such as mathematics, physics,
anthropology, and ethics.
Additionally, it aims to elucidate prospective career trajectories in the field and elucidate the pivotal role played
by computer engineers in contemporary society.

Students are expected to dedicate a total of 150 hours of work between Theroetical Foundations and Practical Skills.

OBJECTIVES

  • To understand the purpose of Computer Engineering, its ultimate goal, and the societal benefits it provides, while
    reflecting on the role each individual wishes to play as an engineer in the future society.
  • To gain knowledge about the pillars on which technological progress must be based, including a historical
    perspective on technological development.
  • To recognize the importance of the human factor in professional practice and foster a sense of service and contribution
    to the common good.
  • To have a global vision of the field of study, its various areas of knowledge, and to distinguish career paths
    and professional profiles in order to question one's own vocation.
  • To provide knowledge about current paradigms, trends in computing, data management, and an introduction to web development.

CONTENTS

PART 1: THEORETICAL FOUNDATIONS

Module 1 - Computer Engineering in Society

  • Historical background and perspectives
  • Definitions and basic concepts
  • Past, present and future of Information and Communication Technology
  • Computer Engineering as an academic discipline and profession

Module 2 - Current paradigms and trends in systems and applications

  • Software development: more than just programming.
  • Structure of computer systems: hardware, software, Operating systems and networks.
  • New paradigms and trends

Module 3 - Information and Data

  • Information and Data: number systems, representation of numeric and alphanumeric information (codes).
  • Data Foundations.

PART 2: PRACTICAL SKILLS

Module 1 - Software repositories.

  • Basic concepts.
  • Repositories: GIT & Github.

Module 2 - Creation of HTML5 web pages

  • Basic concepts
  • The Document Object Model
  • Forms and containers

Module 3 - Design and layout with CSS3

  • Basic concepts.
  • CSS elements.
  • Box and float models.
  • Element positioning.
